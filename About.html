About Me and D.O.T

Felixz A.

My name is Felixz A. I am the inventor of the D.O.T which stands for Directions on Tap. This device is a Non-Surgical Cochlear Implant that can be acquired by contacting us directly. This device came about as I did some studies at Jefferson University. I wanted to find a way for Visually impaired and Blind individuals to safety and more effectively manuever the campus. This device is used like a bluetooth headset so it isn't something that is in the way such as a cane or as distracting as a holding a cell phone. The device works primarily by using commands like "HEY D.O.T direct me to the Downs Audiotorium." It will use googless maps and special software programmed by us to direct you to the building along with in-depth relationships to the surrounding obstacles. D.O.T also has a list of different features such as 'D.O.T how far is my next class from where I am.' D.O.T 'The DEC building is 67 feet. Would you like me to guild you to your next destination?' The real value is that this device is handsfree so it allows you to move freely and not have one or both hands tied up with other devices visually impaired individuals used to use. The D.O.T is designed so that once you wake up in the morning you just grab it put it on and enjoy having your own device that allows you to move freely around campus and be more independent in navigation.
